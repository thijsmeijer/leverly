# Progression Engine

The progression engine should be deterministic, testable, and explainable before any AI-assisted layer is added.

## First Principles

- Safety signals win before overload.
- Pain, poor form, and low readiness should make recommendations conservative.
- Progression should change one major lever at a time by default.
- Explanations should cite the athlete data that caused the recommendation.

## Core Inputs

- Exercise family and variation.
- Reps, sets, holds, tempo, load, assistance, range of motion, and bodyweight.
- Readiness, fatigue, pain, form quality, RPE, and RIR.
- Recent performance history and programmed intent.

## Recommendation Outcomes

- Progress one lever.
- Maintain.
- Reduce volume or intensity.
- Regress variation.
- Deload.
- Request more data when confidence is low.

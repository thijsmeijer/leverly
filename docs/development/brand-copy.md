# Brand And Copy

Leverly is the user-facing product name.

The tagline is: intelligent progression for bodyweight strength.

Use the tagline in places that introduce the product or anchor the main workspace. Do not repeat it inside every panel, card, or button.

## Tone

Leverly copy should be:

- Precise: name the training signal behind guidance, such as readiness, form quality, pain, reps, hold time, leverage, assistance, or load.
- Calm: avoid pressure, hype, blame, and alarmist language.
- Actionable: pair warnings, empty states, and errors with the next useful step.
- Careful: treat pain and readiness as training inputs, not as labels about the athlete.

## Coaching Copy

Recommendations should explain why a change is or is not suggested. Prefer concrete phrases such as "form and readiness evidence" or "before increasing leverage" over generic advice.

Do not claim to identify, treat, or resolve medical conditions. If pain is high or persistent in future product flows, copy should stay conservative and direct the athlete to stop or seek appropriate professional help.

## Error And Empty States

Errors should be short, calm, and useful:

- say what is unavailable;
- name the safest next action;
- avoid blame;
- avoid dead ends.

Empty states should tell the athlete what to do next, not just that there is no data.
